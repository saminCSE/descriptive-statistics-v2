Significant differences were observed between groups in NG length (p=0.003), EX length (p=0.020), and NEX length (p=0.036). The "No side-hole pass" group demonstrated longer NG measurements (488.6±23.4 mm) compared to the side-hole pass group (458.4±23.9 mm), while showing shorter EX (348.1±22.8 mm vs. 368.9±24.5 mm) and NEX measurements (500.0±27.0 mm vs. 523.0±28.9 mm).
Figure 1. Comparison of anatomical measurements between groups showing mean values with standard deviation ranges. Stars (★) indicate statistically significant differences (p < 0.05).
Discussion
Our findings suggest that the traditional NEX measurement method may not adequately predict proper nasogastric tube placement in all patients. The significant variations in anatomical measurements between groups indicate that individual patient characteristics should be considered when determining optimal insertion depth.
